Introduction

One of my best friends, Molly, is starting a magazine called The Well with her sisters (a weird but awesome combination of wellbeing, sisterhood, art, psychological sciences, etc). As a straight white cis woman she's trying her best for the first issue to showcase a wide range of diverse voices, and wants to include an article or feature on any transition stories around the theme/topic of resilience that people are willing to tell.

Molly was the first person I came out as trans to and has been one of my biggest supporters, so of course I want to help her create a piece that supports the trans community, especially when it seems all the articles about us in the media seem to transform transphobia into the nations favourite pastime.

Unfortunately, she likes my tone of writing and thinks my humour is "dry" and "witty" even though most of my writing experience is for technical conference talks (sidenote, I just write the same way I talk to her when trying to deal with her personality which is way too peppy and upbeat for her own good). It might also have something to do with how she "[doesn't] want to butcher a community I only know about through you". How annoyingly considerate of her.
I, being the idiot I am, agreed to this so now I've got to pretend to be a writer or whatever. Molly, the person who actually studied journalism at university, will be assisting me to make sure the end product doesn't end up sounding like hot garbage.

About The Story

The magazine, and it's overall theme of sisterhood, is primarily aimed for an audience of womxn. However I will be writing the article itself with the target audience of the trans community in mind (and possibly publishing it around trans spaces too), meaning all experiences, stories, and perspectives are welcome including non-binary and transmasculine. A story without diversity of thought is only half told.

The theme of the issue is RESILIENCE (apparently the all-caps part is important).

Resilience noun, rɪˈzɪlɪəns

  1. the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties; toughness.

The aim is to elevate trans voices into the wellbeing space by opening up discussion around the theme of resilience during transition and beyond. To showcase the everyday strength of the trans community while navigating a cis world.

What do you interpret resilience to be? What does it mean to you?

End Goal

A feature-length article that will spotlight a significant story or narrative from one or more people, supported by a series of shorter anecdotes, followed by a collection of quotes of peoples thoughts and feelings around resilience.

The Well will take this article and intends to run it across a double page spread - one side for the main stories in feature format and the other for the supporting anecdotes with an artsy collage of quotations.

Expectations

The first issue is scheduled to be published on the spring equinox (20th March 2021), and a final draft of the article will ideally be ready by the first week of March.

You can contribute any way that you feel comfortable. That could be an informal discussion over email, chat or voice call; you can ask for some interview-style questions to use as prompts; if you already know what you want to say you can submit a one-off story or anecdote; you can even send me a long rant if you want to (I know I've needed to in the past).
If there's another way you can think of, let me know! I'll accommodate you to the best of my ability.

Example questions:

  • How would you like to be referred to as? For example, your name, a pseudonym, some really boring title like Anonymous User #3, etc.
  • What are your pronouns?
  • What does the word "resilience" means to you?
  • The story or narrative you want to tell. If you'd like prompts to get you started, try:
    • How are you, as a human being living in this world, resilient?
    • How are you resilient in ways a cis person isn't?
    • How has being trans changed your outlook of what being "tough" is?
    • What makes you feel strong, or makes you feel like you have the strength to overcome things?
    • Is there anything you are now quick to recover from that you weren't so quick at in the past?
    • Has being trans made you more resilient against things you didn't expect? Is there anything that is less difficult now that you are trans?
    • What has been the biggest challenge on your journey so far? How did you, or are still trying to, overcome that?
    • Has there been anything significant that brought you peace, in a time when you needed it?
    • Do you have any self-care practices or rituals in place to help you cultivate resilience?
  • How you think your story would differ if you'd been asked when we weren't a year into a global pandemic and national lockdown?

Guarantees

The story will be checked and edited by other committee members of Trans Pride Brighton (who have kindly volunteered) to make sure no mistakes slip through unintentionally.

A final draft will be sent to everyone involved (that provides a method to contact them) so that they have the option to be removed if they aren't comfortable with what's written.
Or so help me God I will fly to London despite the pandemic and smash Molly's precious Macbook.

No identifying information will be published in the final article without explicit written consent.

GDPR states that personal data (such as email address, etc) must be deleted when it is no longer necessary in relation to the purpose for which it was originally collected/processed.
This means that once the story/article/feature has been written and published, any information that can be used to identify you (other than what you've consented to be in the article itself) or anyone else will be deleted.

Contributing Anonymously

We would love to hear from a wide range of people, but your personal safety should always come first. If you do not want your identity being known by anyone at all, we recommended one of the following:

  • Sending an anonymous email.
  • Completing the anonymous survey made with Google Forms. It's recommended to open the link in an incognito window so you aren't logged into your Google Account when you submit it.
  • Creating a throwaway account on one of the social media platforms to message one of us.
